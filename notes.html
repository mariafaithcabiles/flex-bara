Flexbox Notes

What is Flexbox?

Flexbox is a layout system that lets you easily position items inside a container — horizontally, vertically, centered, or spaced apart. Flexbara uses Flexbox to help you place the capybaras into the right ponds.

1. justify-content

This controls how items are positioned horizontally (left to right). Flexbox Froggy uses this heavily — and so does Flexbara!

Values:

  • flex-start — Items align to the left side.
  • flex-end — Items align to the right side.
  • center — Items align at the center horizontally.
  • space-between — First item at the left, last at the right, equal space in between.
  • space-around — Equal spacing around every item.

Syntax:

justify-content: center;

2. align-items

This controls how items are positioned vertically (top to bottom). Perfect for levels where Flexbara must move up or down.

Values:

  • flex-start — Items go to the top.
  • flex-end — Items go to the bottom.
  • center — Items go to the middle vertically.

Syntax:

align-items: flex-end;

3. flex-direction

Flexbox normally positions items left-to-right. But you can change the direction using flex-direction.

Values:

  • row — Horizontal layout (default)
  • column — Vertical layout (items stack top-to-bottom)

Syntax:

flex-direction: column;

How These Work Together

Many levels require two properties:

  • justify-content for horizontal movement
  • align-items for vertical movement

For vertical layouts, you may also use:

  • flex-direction: column

Master these three, and you'll master Flexbara!
